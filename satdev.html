The mission of the Satellite Development Organization (SatDev) is to engage in research and development of satellite technology and to prepare students for a career in the space industry. I have been involved since my freshman year as a volunteer working for a variety of sub-teams. Sophomore year I developed and taught a university credited course teaching new members. This page will cover the general task I managed; the specific accomplishments I've completed and information on the groups present and future missions.

My Tasks

Infrared Spectroscopy

Infrared spectroscopy is the analysis of infrared light interacting with material. Using a FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared) spectrometer, the emissivity and absorptivity for "in-house" manufactured materials can be found. These test allow for a more qualitative selection process for satellite materials and more accurate thermal simulations. The following are my contributions to these test.

  • Conducted emissivity test on carbon fiber used for solar panels and black anodized aluminum 6061.
  • Created a data acquisition software for pyrometry interferometry procedures using LabVIEW. These test improved test accuracy by 60% and decreased test duration by five hours.
Thermal Simulations
A thermal analysis of a craft is critical to any design considerations. Using the "NX Thermal Space Systems" engine, the satellite was tested under various loads and settings in order to analyze the effects of a space environment on the satellite.

The Missions

LAICE

Lower Atmosphere/Ionosphere Coupling Experiment
Expected Launch: November 2017

The LAICE satellite is studying how gravity waves deliver energy and momentum from the lower atmosphere into the upper atmosphere. Using seven photomultiplier tubes (PMTs) and four optical filters to detect these gravity waves, LAICE's two year mission will work to uncover an answer.

AE199 - Introduction to Satellite Development

The course teaches students the fundamental skills and softwares necessary for working in the lab. I created the lesson plan, course material and taught the course. After the first semester, 5 of my 16 students went off to becoming dominant figures in the SatDev community and all had positive reviews.
